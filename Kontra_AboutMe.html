Judit Kontra

I am a Hungarian girl, who after being introduced to computer science at a STEM camp for girls on the campus of Stanford University for a week (called Tech Trek), decided to become a software engineer. To make this dream a reality, I plan to join and attend computer science related clubs and summer camps, as well as look for internship programs when I am older. You probably think my goal is too general for my age and for living near Silicon Valley, the hotspot of computer science jobs and you are probably right. My goal may be too general, however, around so many talented people, it is challenging to be the best and since I like challenges, I am working my hardest to succeed. During the one week of Tech Trek, with my computer science class, we went on a fieldtrip to Google Headquarters. At Google, first they showed us around in the building we spent the rest of the day, then they let us try Virtual Reality Headsets, and a 3D drawing software for VR, called Tilt Brush. My goal is to work at Google in the future because I admire the working environment, and I also love the softwares they develop, which I would like to be a part of.

In seventh and eighth grade, I was part of the school's leadership class. There were a lot of events we had to plan and activities we had to come up with and plan throughout each year. Sometimes, things were not going as planned, such as when we did not get enough money out of a fundraiser. However, we learned to be ambitious and come up with ideas during reflection time to make the next fundraiser better. In the leadership class, we were also always divided into committees, or groups, so we were never working on an event alone. Therefore, I learned to cooperate well with others.

An mportant achievement I am very proud of is being selected to be one of the eighty girls who could attend Tech Trek out of California. From Tech Trek, I learned some of the basics of MIT App Inventor (and a lot more not-computer-science related things). I also applied and got accepted into the middle school leadership class. From being part of the leadership class, I learned how to work well with people as a team, public speaking, how to plan and organize an event and the qualities of a good leader. Other achievements of mine are that my overall GPA from middle school was a 3.98, and that at the end of eighth grade, I earned an Outstanding Student Award. An activity I currently participate in is a club called Girls Exploring Technology, Science, and Engineering Together (GET SET) where we have a ton of field trip opportunities to sign up for to go to STEM related companies to get to know what they do, what kind of jobs there are, and what each job title is in charge of doing So far from GET SET, I went to SAP, Clorox, and NVIDIA, which taught me a lot of interesting things about the technology they use or create. I had no idea what each of the companies do (other than Clorox making cleaning products, which it's not really just about that), so it was very interesting to learn not just about their technology, but about the company itself.

In conclusion, I am working hard on and aspiring to achieve my goal of becoming a software engineer by developing my computer science related skills, like logic, programming, and working with others.
