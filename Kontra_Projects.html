Scratch Game

August 2018 - September 2018, Computer Science Principles

The objective of this project was to program a game (or story, but we chose game) that includes user interaction, meaning the user has control over what happens in the game, and multiple states, or levels, where the difficulty increases. Since this assignment was a group project, my partner, Arvind and I split the work so that we both worked on the code portion, but I worked more on designing and building the levels, and he worked more on programming the motion of the main sprite.

This project taught me the importance of communication. At first, we had some trouble with comminication. It turned out we both had different views and plans for what the final game would be like. However, after we focused on communication and talked about what we both had in mind, we found a way of incorporating each of our ideas into the game.

Our game is not perfect, of course. It needs improvements and more levels, but it was a good challenge and it taught us both an important lesson. Our game, Flappity Flapper, is found to the right for the client to try out with instructions found below. If you have any ideas of features that you'd like to see in the game, feel free to email me by clicking the email icon at the bottom of the page. Feedback is greatly appreciated.

Instructions to Flappity Flapper

Click the green flag in the corner to start the game. Try to get to the green flag on the screen with the bird without touching the clouds. The bird can be controlled using the up, down, left, and right Arrow Keys. The game will restart the third level when the green flag is touched on Level 3, it's the end of the game.

Have fun playing!

Image Manipulation with Python

March 2018 - April 2018, Computer Science Principles

The objective of this project was to create an algorithm that modifies images according to the needs of the "client" we have chosen out of the four clients we have been provided. The client my partner and I have chosen, Client #1, whom we pretended was a club on our school's campus (Build Club), needed a logo for an organization, company, etc. (in our case a club, of course). The logo we created is shown in the box to the right.

This project taught me that programming isn't just about writing code, but that it's also about the ability to understand code of others and to modify it as needed. The assignments leading up to this project each had new, and new smaller algorithms that demonstrated different kinds of manipulations, which we were allowed to copy, and paste into our algorithm for this project. This required my partner and I to understand the programs in the assignments well enough to know which programs we needed to use, and what we have to add, remove or change to suit the goal of our algorithm, and requirements of the client.

No matter which client we would have chosen, each required a range of options for the final images. To meet this requirement, we made it possible to put the logo anywhere on images by being able to modify the given x- and y-coordinate for the positioning of the logo. In case the logo is hardly visible on images because of the color of the image it is pasted on, we also made a logo that has a white background, so that this wouldn't be a problem. We also added a removable border, and removable dots on the border, of which the client can also modify the color if they even want them to be present on their images. We used our algorithm on actual images that have been taken of members of the Build Club. These images can be viewed in the box to the right with ranges of different outcomes of the algorithm.
